### Lifetime of test results

sponsor: @mbuechse

- premise
  - each test result has an expiration date according to the [lifetime](https://docs.scs.community/standards/scs-0003-v1-sovereign-cloud-standards-yaml#test-case-descriptor) specified in the scope yaml
  - I propose that an ABORT result after a PASS result (for the same testcase) should effectively be counted as PASS (otherwise the original PASS would turn into MISS after expiration, which could in the worst case lead to the subject losing certification)
  - reasoning: ABORT means we tried to prove that the subject doesn't comply, and we failed; so we should assume that it complies (in dubio pro reo)
  - case in point: Keystone no longer issues EC2 credentials for 'restricted' application credentials (which is correct!), and it's our fault that we don't use 'unrestricted' application credentials everywhere!
  - maybe also turn ABORT after FAIL into FAIL?
- discussion
  - it needs to be visible immediately for the engineer at Forum SCS Standards that something is fishy
  - the user tends to want to rely on a green badge, so they need some kind of indication as well, for instance a date of when the tests were last unequivocally passed
  - idea: indicate certainty, i.e., distinguish between "we are certain that it's a PASS", "we aren't certain" and "we are certain that it's a FAIL"
  - take discussion into JF between @depressiveRobot and @mbuechse
