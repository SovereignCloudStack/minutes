### How to handle Mergers & Acquisitions

sponsor: @depressiveRobot

- So far we've not regulated the cases of M&A
- Now we actually have a case of an existing, certified SCS-Integrator which has been acquired by another member of the Forum
- Transfer of certificate and remaining valid?
  - Is M&A the legal successor?
  - Layman's legal assessment: By default, the acquiring company takes over the rights and responsibilities of the acquired company (= legal successor) in which case all certs would remain valid if we don't have an explicit diverging rule.
- Discussion what happens when acquiring company lays off all qualified persons
  - This can happen even without M&A
- Medium-term evolution: Possibly have individual certifications (qualification) as requirement for company certifications?
- Short term suggestion: Add to SCS-0007: "The qualification must remain in place for as long as the certificate is valid."
  - maybe have this reviewed by a law expert before
  	- overkill?
- Alternatively just wait for expiration...
- _AI: @depressiveRobot creates PR to add short term suggestion_
